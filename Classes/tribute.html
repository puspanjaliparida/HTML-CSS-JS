Rohit Sharma

Indian Cricketer

About Rohit Sharma

Rohit Sharma, born on April 30, 1987, in Bansod, Nagpur, Maharashtra, India, has emerged as one of the most talented and successful cricketers in Indian cricket history. His journey from a young prodigy to becoming the vice-captain of the Indian national team reflects his dedication, skill, and consistency in the sport. Rohit is renowned for his elegant and effortless batting style, often blending classical strokes with power hitting, making him a formidable force across all formats of the game. His ability to adapt to different conditions and situations on the field has earned him accolades and numerous records in international cricket. Rohit's leadership skills have also been showcased as he has captained the Indian team in various matches and tournaments with success, further solidifying his position as a key figure in Indian cricket. Beyond his individual achievements, Rohit Sharma's contributions to team victories and his role as a mentor to younger players continue to inspire cricket enthusiasts worldwide.

Career Achievements

  1. International Debut: Rohit Sharma made his international debut for India in 2007 in an ODI against Ireland. He later made his Test debut in 2013 against the West Indies.
  2. Batting Style: He is a right-handed batsman and is known for his elegant strokeplay, especially in limited-overs cricket.
  3. Captaincy: Rohit has also showcased his leadership abilities as the captain of the Indian cricket team in limited-overs formats, including leading the team to victories in major tournaments like the ICC Champions Trophy and Asia Cup.
  4. IPL: Rohit Sharma is renowned as a five-time IPL winning captain with the Mumbai Indians, showcasing his exceptional leadership in the Indian Premier League.
  5. Records: He holds several records in cricket, including the highest individual score in One Day Internationals (ODIs) with 264 runs against Sri Lanka.
